I am an Assistant Professor at NIT, Anan College, Japan, researching in the field of differential geometry. I am interested in transformation theory of isothermic surfaces in Cayley–Klein geometries such as Lie-sphere geometry, Möbius geometry, and Laguerre geometry, and am also working on discrete differential geometry. Research Interests

Areas of Study

Isothermic Surfaces & Transformation Theory

Studying Darboux, Ribaucour, and related transformations of isothermic surfaces in Cayley–Klein geometries, including Lie-sphere geometry, Möbius geometry, and Laguerre geometry.

Zero Mean Curvature Surfaces

Classification and geometric properties of surfaces with zero mean curvature in isotropic space, with particular attention to curvature line geometry and invariants under decomposition.

Integrable Systems

Application of integrable systems methods — loop groups, Lax representations, DPW method — to the geometry of surfaces and their transformations in curved ambient spaces.

Discrete Differential Geometry

Constructing discrete analogues of classical smooth surface theory, including Weierstrass-type representations for discrete constant mean curvature surfaces in isotropic space.

Mathematical Visualizations

Research Surfaces

Bonnet-type surface in ℝ³

A surface in Euclidean 3-space arising from Bonnet-type isothermic transformation theory, exhibiting characteristic periodic symmetry and self-intersection patterns along the central plane.

Enneper-type surface in ℝ³

A surface of Enneper-type in Euclidean 3-space, displaying the characteristic petal-like lobes and singular pinch-point structure of this classical family with curvature line parametrization.
